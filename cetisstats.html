Statistics

Statistical Methods

A full suite of advanced methods for hypothesis testing and point estimation in environmental toxicology.

Overview

Two Pillars of Analysis

CETIS provides comprehensive statistical tools organized around two core analytical approaches.

Hypothesis Testing

Determine NOEL/LOEL (NOEC/LOEC) values through parametric and non-parametric statistical comparisons between control and treatment groups.

Point Estimation

Calculate EC/LC/IC values with confidence intervals through linear and non-linear regression models, including Spearman-Karber, Trimmed Spearman-Karber, and more.

Regression

Special

Additional Methods

EPA TST Method

The U.S. EPA Test for Significant Toxicity (TST) using Welch's t Test — a bioequivalence approach that controls the false negative rate for more reliable toxicity determinations.

Ratio & Comparison Tests

Robertson and Preisler ratio test and the Zajdlik two-sample Z test for comparing two different point estimates with statistical rigor.

Spearman-Karber Method

LC50/EC50 estimates with 95% confidence intervals, including the Trimmed Spearman-Karber variant for improved robustness.

NOEL/LOEL Calculations

Both NOEL/LOEL (NOEC/LOEC) calculations can be immediately followed by automated point estimate calculations for a complete analytical workflow.

General

General Questions

CETIS (Comprehensive Environmental Toxicity Information System) is a professional-level toxicity data analysis and database application developed by Tidepool Scientific, LLC. It is a Microsoft Access–based application designed to thoroughly analyze, maintain, and organize complex and heterogeneous environmental toxicity data.

You will need to know almost nothing of the Structured Query Language (SQL) because CETIS does all the work for you behind the scenes. Although you will need to know very little of the Access application to successfully use CETIS, the more you learn about Access, the more you can apply to using CETIS.

ToxCalc was the original spreadsheet-based toxicity analysis application developed by Tidepool Scientific, which became the best-selling software of its kind with over 500 registered users. CETIS is its successor — a full database application built on the Microsoft Access platform that provides far more comprehensive data management, analysis, and reporting capabilities.

CETIS efficiently stores and processes data derived from freshwater, estuarine, marine, and terrestrial toxicity tests performed on various environmental media such as effluents, ambient water, reference toxicants, and sediment samples or preparations. It is also easily adapted to toxicity tests performed on other environmental media such as soils or aerosols.

Analysis

Data Analysis

The decision flowcharts that must be followed during the analysis of toxicity data can be complicated and at times overwhelming. CETIS implements decision-making logic that makes most, if not all, decisions for you in terms of selecting the correct method and verifying that all assumptions are met. You can also override any software-made decision and manually select methods and options of your choice.

Yes. Since many federal and state government programs have different decision-making rules guiding the selection of the appropriate statistical methods, CETIS has a system to quickly change the decision rules depending on which jurisdiction governs the testing. This multi-jurisdictional support allows labs to efficiently handle samples governed by different regulatory frameworks.

CETIS offers a full suite of advanced statistical methods for hypothesis testing and point estimation. This includes linear regression with 4 MLE models (Probit, Logit, Gompertz, Angle), non-linear regression with 41 models, the U.S. EPA TST method, Spearman-Karber, and comprehensive residual analysis tests. See our Statistical Methods page for full details.
